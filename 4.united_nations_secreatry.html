United Nations Secretary

In September 1961, United Nations Secretary-General Dag Hammarskjöld was killed in a plane crash en route to Congo. Within two weeks, the United States and the Soviet Union had agreed to appoint Thant as the Acting Secretary-General for the remainder of Hammarskjöld's term. However, the two superpowers spent another four weeks arguing over the details of his appointment. On November 3, 1961, the Security Council recommended Thant in Resolution 168, and the General Assembly voted unanimously to appoint Thant to a term of office ending on April 10, 1963.

During his first term, he was widely credited for his role in defusing the Cuban Missile Crisis and for ending the civil war in the Congo. He also said that he wanted to ease tensions between major powers while serving at the UN. His election to the position came about under unique circumstances, and his tenure marked a significant period in the history of the United Nations.

Background and Election

U Thant's election as Secretary-General was unprecedented. He was serving as the UN's Acting Secretary-General following the sudden death of Dag Hammarskjöld in a plane crash in 1961. U Thant, then the Burmese representative to the UN, was appointed as Acting Secretary-General to ensure continuity during a critical time. In 1962, U Thant was formally elected as Secretary-General by the UN General Assembly. His election was notable as he was the first Asian and the first non-Westerner to hold the position.

Tenure and Accomplishments

U Thant's tenure saw several important international developments, including the Cuban Missile Crisis and the Congo Crisis. He played a mediating role in these and other conflicts, advocating for peaceful resolutions. He worked to advance decolonization and promote self-determination for newly independent nations. U Thant played a pivotal role during the Cold War, maintaining the UN's neutrality and providing a platform for diplomatic negotiations between the United States and the Soviet Union. He supported peacekeeping efforts and crisis management in various regions, including the Middle East and Cyprus.

Promotion of Diplomacy and Peace

U Thant was a strong advocate for diplomacy and peaceful conflict resolution. He promoted the idea that the UN should act as a neutral intermediary and provide a platform for dialogue between conflicting parties. His "good offices" diplomacy was instrumental in defusing tensions and preventing the escalation of conflicts during his tenure.

Legacy

U Thant's calm and diplomatic approach earned him respect and admiration on the international stage. His commitment to maintaining the UN's credibility and effectiveness was widely recognized. He declined to seek a second term as Secretary-General and retired from the position in 1971. U Thant's legacy is marked by his efforts to promote peaceful coexistence, diplomatic solutions, and the principles of the United Nations.
